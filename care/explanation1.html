ANALYSIS RESULT

Based on the data and the image, our system detect abnormality. It is highly likely that you have Ductal Carcinoma InSitu (DCIS).

CARE is only 98% accurate. Therefore, there is a 2% chance that our assessment is incorrect.

DCIS is an early form of breast cancer. DCIS means the cells that line the milk ducts of the breast have become cancer, but they have not spread into surrounding breast tissue.

Please note, that DCIS is considered non-invasive or pre-invasive breast cancer. DCIS can’t spread outside the breast, but it still needs to be treated because it can sometimes go on to become invasive breast cancer (which can spread).

In some cases, DCIS will never develop further or grows so slowly that it would never cause harm during that person’s lifetime. Although the size and grade of the DCIS can help predict if it will become invasive, there is currently no way of knowing if this will happen. Please talk with your doctor to make sure of it.

If you want to read more about DCIS, here is the complete information from Cancer Society and Cancer Research UK.

Treatment

Because there’s no way of knowing when or if DCIS will become invasive, treatment is usually recommended. It is possible that this may lead to unnecessary or overtreatment for some people.

Surgery is nearly always the first treatment for DCIS. This may be breast-conserving surgery or a mastectomy. Your surgeon might recommend that you have a particular surgery or they might give you a choice of operations.

If you want to read more about the surgery, here is the complete information about breast-conserving surgery and mastectomy.

What Next

Based on your current result, it is highly suggested for you to contact your doctor for further assessment. This is because more tests are needed to assess and finalised the diagnosis.

Further Support

Being told you have DCIS can be a difficult and worrying time. Everyone reacts differently to their diagnosis and have their own way of coping. Although DCIS is an early form of breast cancer with a very good prognosis, people understandably may feel very anxious and frightened by the diagnosis. People can often struggle to come to terms with being offered treatments such as a mastectomy, at the same time as being told their DCIS may never do them any harm.

If you want to talk, don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it to people around you. You can also call our Helpline on 0808 800 6000.

How CARE analyse my result?

CARE is using a cutting-edge algorithm that is specialised in recognising abnormal breast image based on a thermal scan. CARE's algorithm had went through cycles of process of training and testing using a large amount of thermal scan data. High likelihood assessments are given because there is some similarity between your thermal image and the breast cancer images in CARE’s database.

What is the accuracy of CARE?

The latest research proves that CARE can detect 98% of breast cancer. Therefore, there is a 2% chance that our assessment is incorrect. That means in every 100 assessments, there is 2 assessments that give a wrong result. This accuracy percentage is obtained from testing and comparison between data.

What data is used?

CARE's database is a combined database of thermal image records from several big hospitals around the world. Two of the hospitals are Hammersmith Hospital, UK and Ewha Women University Hospital, South Korea.

How CARE use my personal data?

CARE does not use your personal data and not giving away to any third party. CARE is Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) compliant and General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) ready.
